What Is a "Cookie"?

We often place a small file known as a "cookie" on Your computer's hard drive. A cookie may contain information that allows Us to track Your path through the Sites and to determine whether You have visited Us before. However, unless You provide Us with personally identifiable information, the cookies will not contain any information that would allow Us to personally identify You. If You do provide personally identifiable information to Us, the cookie is tied to this information so that Our systems will recognize You each time You use the Sites. In that way, We can save Your preferences from visit to visit and present You with a customized version of the Sites every time You visit. Our cookies cannot be used to read data from Your hard drive and cannot retrieve information from any other cookies created by other sites. We use cookies in this manner to help Us understand how Users use the Sites, and to help Us to improve the Sites.

For more details on cookies and similar technologies, please visit www.allaboutcookies.org.

Cookies and Other Technologies

Different Durations of Cookies

Cookies can be stored on your computer for different periods of time. "Session cookie" only last for as long as the browser session and are deleted automatically once You close your browser. "Persistent cookies" cookies survive after your browser is closed until a defined expiration date set in the cookie (as determined by the party placing it), and help recognize your computer when You open your browser and browse the Internet again.

First Party and Third Party Cookies

First party cookies are placed on a website directly by the owner of the Site, in this case, BackThenHistory. Third party cookies are placed on a website by third party providers (for example, by companies that provide advertising tracking or web analytics services) and by advertisers on the Site. We use both first-party cookies as well as third-party cookies.

Pixel Tags

We also use pixel tags (usually in combination with cookies), from the third parties, to get information about your usage of the Sites and about your interaction with email or other communications. Pixel tags are a technology similar to cookies that can be embedded in online content or within the body of an email for the purpose of tracking activity on websites (for example, to know when content has been shown to You), or to know when You have viewed particular content or a particular email message.
